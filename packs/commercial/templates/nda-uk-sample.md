## MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

**This Agreement is made on [DATE]**

**BETWEEN:**

1. **[PARTY A NAME]**, a company incorporated in [ENGLAND/SCOTLAND/WALES/NORTHERN IRELAND] with company number [NUMBER] and whose registered office is at [ADDRESS] ("**Party A**"); and

2. **[PARTY B NAME]**, a company incorporated in [ENGLAND/SCOTLAND/WALES/NORTHERN IRELAND] with company number [NUMBER] and whose registered office is at [ADDRESS] ("**Party B**").

Each a "**Party**" and together the "**Parties**".

---

## BACKGROUND

A. The Parties wish to explore a potential business relationship (the "**Purpose**").

B. In connection with the Purpose, each Party may disclose to the other Party certain Confidential Information (as defined below).

C. The Parties wish to protect the confidentiality of such Confidential Information on the terms set out in this Agreement.

---

## AGREED TERMS

### 1. DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION

**1.1** In this Agreement, the following definitions apply:

- **"Confidential Information"** means all information (whether written, oral, visual, electronic or in any other form) disclosed by one Party (the "**Disclosing Party**") to the other Party (the "**Receiving Party**") in connection with the Purpose, including but not limited to:
  - (a) technical data, trade secrets, know-how, research, product plans, services, customer lists and customers;
  - (b) markets, software, developments, inventions, processes, designs, drawings, engineering, hardware configuration information;
  - (c) financial information, business plans, strategies, and forecasts;
  - (d) any other information marked as "Confidential" or which a reasonable person would consider confidential.

- **"Data Protection Laws"** means the UK Data Protection Act 2018, UK GDPR, and any other applicable data protection or privacy legislation.

**1.2** References to a "**company**" include any company, corporation, or other body corporate, wherever incorporated.

---

### 2. OBLIGATIONS OF CONFIDENTIALITY

**2.1** Each Party agrees to:

(a) keep all Confidential Information strictly confidential;

(b) not use the Confidential Information for any purpose other than the Purpose without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party;

(c) not disclose, publish, or disseminate the Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party;

(d) protect the Confidential Information using the same degree of care as it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, but in any event no less than a reasonable standard of care;

(e) only disclose the Confidential Information to its employees, officers, representatives, or advisers ("**Representatives**") who have a legitimate need to know the Confidential Information for the Purpose and who have been informed of the confidential nature of the information and are bound by obligations of confidentiality no less onerous than those set out in this Agreement.

**2.2** Each Party shall be responsible for any breach of this Agreement by its Representatives.

---

### 3. PERMITTED DISCLOSURES

**3.1** The obligations in Clause 2 shall not apply to Confidential Information that:

(a) is or becomes publicly available other than through breach of this Agreement;

(b) was lawfully in the possession of the Receiving Party before disclosure by the Disclosing Party;

(c) is lawfully obtained by the Receiving Party from a third party without breach of any confidentiality obligation;

(d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Confidential Information; or

(e) is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, court order, or governmental authority, provided that the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice of such requirement and cooperates with the Disclosing Party's reasonable efforts to obtain a protective order or confidential treatment.

---

### 4. DATA PROTECTION

**4.1** To the extent that Confidential Information includes personal data (as defined in Data Protection Laws), the Parties agree to comply with all applicable Data Protection Laws when processing such personal data.

**4.2** Each Party shall implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect any personal data disclosed under this Agreement.

---

### 5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

**5.1** All Confidential Information remains the property of the Disclosing Party. Nothing in this Agreement grants any license or right to use any intellectual property rights of the Disclosing Party.

---

### 6. RETURN OR DESTRUCTION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

**6.1** Upon termination of this Agreement or upon request by the Disclosing Party, the Receiving Party shall promptly:

(a) return to the Disclosing Party all documents and materials containing Confidential Information; and

(b) permanently delete or destroy all copies (including electronic copies) of Confidential Information in its possession or control.

**6.2** The Receiving Party shall certify in writing to the Disclosing Party that it has complied with Clause 6.1.

**6.3** Notwithstanding Clause 6.1, the Receiving Party may retain copies of Confidential Information to the extent required by law or professional regulations, subject to continued compliance with this Agreement.

---

### 7. NO WARRANTY

**7.1** All Confidential Information is provided "as is" without any warranty, express or implied, as to its accuracy, completeness, or fitness for any particular purpose.

---

### 8. NO OBLIGATION TO PROCEED

**8.1** Nothing in this Agreement shall create any obligation on either Party to proceed with any transaction or relationship.

**8.2** Neither Party shall have any liability to the other Party arising out of or in connection with the Purpose or negotiations, except for breaches of this Agreement.

---

### 9. TERM AND TERMINATION

**9.1** This Agreement shall commence on the date of this Agreement and continue for a period of [2/3/5] years unless terminated earlier by either Party giving [30/60/90] days' written notice.

**9.2** The obligations of confidentiality under Clause 2 shall survive termination of this Agreement for a period of [3/5/7] years.

---

### 10. REMEDIES

**10.1** Each Party acknowledges that damages may not be an adequate remedy for breach of this Agreement and that the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek equitable relief, including injunction and specific performance, without proof of actual damage.

---

### 11. GENERAL

**11.1 Entire Agreement:** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes all previous agreements and understandings relating to its subject matter.

**11.2 Variation:** No variation of this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing and signed by both Parties.

**11.3 Assignment:** Neither Party may assign or transfer any of its rights or obligations under this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other Party.

**11.4 Severability:** If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

**11.5 Waiver:** No failure or delay by either Party in exercising any right or remedy under this Agreement shall constitute a waiver of that right or remedy.

**11.6 Counterparts:** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument.

---

### 12. GOVERNING LAW AND JURISDICTION

**12.1** This Agreement and any dispute or claim arising out of or in connection with it or its subject matter shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the law of **England and Wales** [or Scotland/Northern Ireland as applicable].

**12.2** The Parties irrevocably agree that the courts of **England and Wales** shall have exclusive jurisdiction to settle any dispute or claim arising out of or in connection with this Agreement or its subject matter.

## Usage Notes

### Customization Points

1. **Clause 9.1 - Duration:** Choose appropriate term (2/3/5 years) and notice period (30/60/90 days)
2. **Clause 9.2 - Survival Period:** Choose how long confidentiality survives termination (3/5/7 years typical)
3. **Clause 12 - Governing Law:** Select England and Wales, Scotland, or Northern Ireland based on your business location
4. **Background:** Customize the "Purpose" to describe your specific business relationship

### When to Use This Template

✅ Suitable for:
- Business discussions between UK companies
- Technology partnerships
- M&A due diligence
- Supplier/customer relationships
- Joint ventures and collaborations

❌ NOT suitable for:
- Employment relationships (use Employment Contract template)
- Consumer contracts
- International transactions without UK nexus
- Situations requiring one-way confidentiality (use Unilateral NDA instead)

### Legal Review Checklist

Before signing:
- [ ] Verify all party details (company numbers, registered addresses)
- [ ] Confirm jurisdiction matches your business location
- [ ] Ensure duration and survival periods are appropriate
- [ ] Review permitted disclosure exceptions
- [ ] Confirm IP ownership clauses meet your needs
- [ ] Have a UK solicitor review if:
  - High-value transaction
  - Complex IP involved
  - Cross-border elements
  - Regulated industry (finance, healthcare, etc.)
