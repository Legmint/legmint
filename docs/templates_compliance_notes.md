## GLOBAL-EN (Baseline)

### Overview
The GLOBAL-EN baseline templates are drafted in plain English following international best practices. They are intentionally jurisdiction-neutral to provide a foundation that can be adapted for specific jurisdictions via overlays.

### Key Characteristics
- **Language**: English
- **Legal Style**: International commercial contracting style
- **Currency**: USD by default (customizable)
- **Governing Law**: Placeholder - must be specified by user
- **Venue**: Placeholder - must be specified by user

### Limitations
- **Not Enforceable Standalone**: GLOBAL-EN templates MUST be combined with a jurisdiction overlay for enforceability.
- **No Local Statutory References**: Does not cite local statutes or regulations.
- **Generic Corporate Forms**: Uses generic terms like "company" and "shares" without jurisdiction-specific corporate form terminology.

### Use Cases
- Starting point for custom agreements
- Internal planning and drafting
- Multi-jurisdictional deals (with local counsel review)

## United Kingdom (UK)

### Applicable Law
- **Companies Act 2006** (CA 2006)
- **Insolvency Act 1986**
- **Employment Rights Act 1996**
- **UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018**
- **Financial Services and Markets Act 2000** (if FCA-regulated)
- **Common law** (case law precedents)

### Corporate Structure
- **Ltd** (Private Limited Company) - most common for startups
- **PLC** (Public Limited Company) - for listed companies
- **LLP** (Limited Liability Partnership)

**Key Points**:
- Share capital concepts (ordinary shares, preference shares)
- Articles of Association govern internal affairs
- Companies House registration and filing requirements
- Pre-emption rights on share issues (CA 2006 ss.561-577)

### Fundraising Pack Compliance

#### Founders' Agreement / Shareholders' Agreement
- **Pre-emption Rights**: May be statutory (CA 2006) or contractual. Ensure Articles permit exclusion/modification.
- **Drag-Along/Tag-Along**: Enforceable if properly documented. Must not breach minority protection rules (unfair prejudice, CA 2006 s.994).
- **Leaver Provisions**: "Bad leaver" provisions must be reasonable. Restraint of trade clauses subject to reasonableness test under common law.
- **Vesting**: Employee share schemes may require EMI (Enterprise Management Incentive) or CSOP (Company Share Option Plan) approval for tax efficiency.
- **Board Composition**: Subject to CA 2006 director duties (ss.171-177).

#### SAFE / Convertible Instruments
- **Legal Status**: Treated as contractual right to future shares, not a security for FCA purposes (if under prospectus thresholds).
- **Tax**: EIS/SEIS relief may be available for qualifying investors.
- **Consideration**: Shares must be issued for adequate consideration (CA 2006 s.580).
- **Articles**: Must permit creation of preference shares and conversions.

### SaaS Pack Compliance

#### SaaS Subscription Agreement
- **B2B vs B2C**: B2B terms are generally enforceable. B2C requires compliance with Consumer Rights Act 2015 and Unfair Terms Regulations.
- **Limitation of Liability**: Subject to Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977 reasonableness test. Cannot exclude liability for death/personal injury, fraud, or gross negligence.
- **Termination**: Notice periods must be reasonable. For B2C, comply with Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013 (14-day cancellation right).

#### Data Processing Agreement (DPA)
- **UK GDPR**: Post-Brexit, UK GDPR (EU GDPR as retained in UK law) applies.
- **ICO**: Information Commissioner's Office is the supervisory authority.
- **International Transfers**: Use International Data Transfer Addendum (IDTA) to EU SCCs for transfers outside UK. Transfer Risk Assessments required per ICO guidance.
- **Breach Notification**: 72 hours to ICO (Article 33 UK GDPR).
- **Fines**: Up to £17.5 million or 4% of annual turnover, whichever is higher.

**Key Differences from EU**:
- UK has separate adequacy regime (s.17A DPA 2018).
- IDTA v2.0 replaces EU SCCs for UK transfers.
- ICO guidance diverges from EDPB on some points.

### Practical Guidance
- All company documents should reference Companies House registration number.
- Use "Ltd" or "Limited" in company name (mandatory for ltd companies).
- Ensure Articles of Association align with shareholders' agreement provisions.
- For employee agreements, check Employment Rights Act compliance (unfair dismissal, notice periods, restrictive covenants).

## United States - Delaware (US-DE)

### Applicable Law
- **Delaware General Corporation Law (DGCL)** - Title 8 of Delaware Code
- **Securities Act of 1933** (federal) - Regulation D exemptions
- **Securities Exchange Act of 1934** (federal)
- **Uniform Commercial Code (UCC)** (as adopted in Delaware)

### Corporate Structure
- **C-Corporation** - standard for venture-backed startups
- **LLC** (Limited Liability Company) - alternative structure
- **Certificate of Incorporation** + **Bylaws** govern internal affairs

**Key Points**:
- Delaware is the dominant jurisdiction for US startups (flexible corporate law, specialized Court of Chancery).
- Board has significant authority; shareholder rights defined in Certificate and stockholder agreements.
- Series of preferred stock with specific rights (liquidation preference, anti-dilution, protective provisions).

### Fundraising Pack Compliance

#### Founders' Agreement / Stockholders' Agreement
- **Enforceability**: Generally enforceable under Delaware contract law if properly drafted.
- **Drag-Along**: Permitted; ensure Certificate authorizes necessary corporate actions.
- **Voting Agreements**: Enforceable (DGCL §218).
- **Restrictive Covenants**: Subject to reasonableness standard. Non-competes enforceable in Delaware (unlike California).
- **Board Rights**: Investors commonly have board seat appointment rights and observer rights.

#### SAFE / Convertible Note
- **Legal Status**: SAFE is a contract, not a security itself (no debt). Shares issued upon conversion are securities.
- **Securities Compliance**: Ensure Regulation D exemption (Rule 506(b) or 506(c)) for issuance to accredited investors.
- **Blue Sky**: May require state securities law compliance (though often exempted under Reg D).
- **Certificate Requirements**: Certificate of Incorporation must authorize sufficient shares and permit preference shares with specified rights.

#### Liquidation Preferences
- **DGCL §242**: Amendment of Certificate requires board + stockholder approval.
- **Participating vs Non-Participating**: Clearly define in Certificate and stockholder agreement.
- **Multiple Series**: Later series (e.g., Series B) typically have seniority over earlier series (Series A) in liquidation waterfall.

### SaaS Pack Compliance

#### SaaS Subscription Agreement
- **Enforceability**: Generally enforceable under Delaware or other state law (specify in choice of law clause).
- **Limitation of Liability**: Generally enforceable for B2B; some states prohibit for consumers.
- **Clickwrap/Browsewrap**: Ensure proper formation (mutual assent). Clickwrap generally enforceable; browsewrap more challenging.
- **Data Security**: Consider state data breach notification laws (all 50 states have them).

#### Data Processing Agreement
- **No Federal Privacy Law**: US lacks comprehensive federal privacy law (as of 2025).
- **State Laws**: Comply with California CCPA/CPRA, Virginia VCDPA, Colorado CPA, Connecticut CTDPA, Utah UCPA (and others enacted).
- **GDPR Applicability**: If processing data of EU data subjects, GDPR applies (US-EU Data Privacy Framework for adequacy).
- **Sector-Specific**: HIPAA (healthcare), GLBA (financial), COPPA (children).

**For B2B SaaS**:
- Processor agreements under state privacy laws (CCPA, VCDPA, etc.).
- Standard Contractual Clauses or Data Privacy Framework for EU/UK data.

### Practical Guidance
- Ensure Certificate of Incorporation filed with Delaware Secretary of State.
- Maintain Registered Agent in Delaware.
- File annual franchise tax report.
- Stock ledger and capitalization table must be accurate and up-to-date.
- For SAFEs: post-money valuation cap is standard in Silicon Valley (Y Combinator form).

## Germany (DE)

### Applicable Law
- **GmbH-Gesetz (GmbHG)** - GmbH (limited liability company) law
- **Aktiengesetz (AktG)** - AG (stock corporation) law
- **Bürgerliches Gesetzbuch (BGB)** - Civil Code (contract law)
- **Handelsgesetzbuch (HGB)** - Commercial Code
- **Insolvenzordnung (InsO)** - Insolvency law
- **EU GDPR** (directly applicable) + **Bundesdatenschutzgesetz (BDSG)** (German DPA)

### Corporate Structure
- **GmbH** (Gesellschaft mit beschränkter Haftung) - most common for startups (equivalent to Ltd/LLC)
- **UG (haftungsbeschränkt)** - "mini-GmbH" with lower capital requirement (€1 minimum)
- **AG** (Aktiengesellschaft) - stock corporation for larger companies/IPOs

**Key Points**:
- **Gesellschaftsvertrag** (articles of association) govern GmbH.
- Notarization required for share transfers (§15 GmbHG).
- Minimum capital: €25,000 for GmbH; €50,000 for AG.
- Trade register (Handelsregister) registration mandatory.

### Fundraising Pack Compliance

#### Founders' Agreement / Shareholders' Agreement
- **Form**: Amendments to Gesellschaftsvertrag require notarization.
- **Pre-emption Rights**: Can be included in Gesellschaftsvertrag; enforceable if properly drafted.
- **Drag-Along**: Enforceable if included in articles or contractual agreement.
- **Restrictive Covenants**: Non-compete clauses subject to §§74-75a HGB (commercial agents) and BGB reasonableness principles. Generally enforceable if reasonable in scope, duration, and geography.
- **Vesting**: Not a statutory concept; implemented contractually via call options or transfer restrictions.

#### SAFE / Convertible Instruments
- **Legal Form**: SAFEs are not common in Germany. Similar instruments:
  - **Wandeldarlehen** (convertible loan) - debt instrument convertible to shares.
  - **Stille Beteiligung** (silent partnership) - participation in profits without share ownership.
- **Prospectus Regulation**: EU Prospectus Regulation applies (exempt if < €8M over 12 months or < 150 investors).
- **Capital Requirements**: Shares must be issued for adequate consideration (§§19, 56 GmbHG; §§36-37 AktG).
- **Notarization**: Transfer of GmbH shares requires notarial deed (§15 GmbHG).

### SaaS Pack Compliance

#### SaaS Subscription Agreement
- **BGB Contract Law**: §§145-157 BGB (offer, acceptance, formation).
- **AGB (Standard Terms)**: §§305-310 BGB regulate standard form contracts. Unfair terms may be void.
- **B2B**: Generally enforceable if commercially reasonable.
- **B2C**: Consumer protection applies (Verbraucherschutzgesetze); 14-day cooling-off period for distance contracts.
- **Liability**: Cannot exclude liability for gross negligence (**grobe Fahrlässigkeit**) or willful misconduct (**Vorsatz**).

#### Data Processing Agreement
- **EU GDPR**: Applies directly (no national implementation needed).
- **BDSG**: Supplements GDPR with German-specific rules (e.g., employee data protection §26 BDSG).
- **Supervisory Authority**: Varies by state (Bundesland). Federal: Bundesbeauftragter für den Datenschutz und die Informationsfreiheit (BfDI).
- **Transfer Mechanisms**: EU SCCs for transfers outside EU/EEA; adequacy decisions (e.g., for UK, under review for US).
- **Breach Notification**: 72 hours to supervisory authority (Article 33 GDPR).

### Practical Guidance
- All German language translations recommended for enforceability in German courts.
- Gesellschaftsvertrag should expressly permit preference shares/podíly (Vorzugsanteile).
- Register GmbH with Handelsregister; publish in Bundesanzeiger.
- For employees: works council (Betriebsrat) rights may apply if ≥5 employees.
- Tax: Körperschaftsteuer (corporate tax) ~30%; trade tax (Gewerbesteuer) ~15%; solidarity surcharge (Solidaritätszuschlag) 5.5%.

## France (FR)

### Applicable Law
- **Code de commerce** - Commercial Code (company law)
  - Book II: Commercial companies (Livre II)
  - Articles L.227-1 et seq. (SAS)
  - Articles L.223-1 et seq. (SARL)
- **Code civil** - Civil Code (contract law, obligations)
- **Code monétaire et financier** - Financial and Monetary Code
- **EU GDPR** (directly applicable) + national implementing law
- **Code du travail** - Labor Code

### Corporate Structure
- **SAS** (Société par Actions Simplifiée) - most flexible for startups; no minimum capital
- **SARL** (Société à Responsabilité Limitée) - traditional limited liability company
- **SA** (Société Anonyme) - for larger companies/IPOs (minimum capital €37,000)

**Key Points**:
- **Statuts** (bylaws) govern SAS/SARL.
- Président and other officers (gérant for SARL) manage company.
- SAS allows preference shares (actions de préférence) with special rights (Art. L.228-11 et seq. Code de commerce).
- SARL restricted in share class flexibility.

### Fundraising Pack Compliance

#### Founders' Agreement / Shareholders' Agreement
- **Pacte d'Actionnaires**: Shareholders' agreement ("pacte d'actionnaires") enforceable under French contract law.
- **Pre-emption Rights**: Statutory pre-emption for SARL (Art. L.223-14 Code de commerce); contractual for SAS.
- **Drag-Along**: Enforceable if included in statuts or pacte.
- **Clauses Léonines**: Prohibition on clauses that allocate all profits to one partner or exclude a partner from all profits/losses (Art. 1844-1 Code civil). Carefully draft liquidation preferences to avoid this.
- **Non-Compete**: Enforceable if reasonable in scope, duration, and geography. For employees, requires compensation.

#### SAFE / Convertible Instruments
- **Rare in France**: SAFEs not commonly used. Alternatives:
  - **Obligations Convertibles en Actions (OCA)** - convertible bonds (debt).
  - **BSA** (Bons de Souscription d'Actions) - warrants.
  - **BSPCE** (Bons de Souscription de Parts de Créateur d'Entreprise) - founder/employee warrants with tax benefits.
- **Prospectus**: EU Prospectus Regulation applies.
- **AMF**: Autorité des marchés financiers regulates public offerings.
- **Capital Contributions**: Arts. L.223-7 (SARL), L.227-1 (SAS) - shares must be issued for consideration.

### SaaS Pack Compliance

#### SaaS Subscription Agreement
- **Code civil**: Contract formation (Arts. 1101 et seq.).
- **Clauses Abusives**: Unfair terms in B2C contracts (Code de la consommation, Art. L.212-1). Black list and gray list of prohibited clauses.
- **B2B**: Generally enforceable if commercially reasonable.
- **Liability**: Cannot exclude liability for **faute lourde** (gross negligence) or **dol** (willful misconduct).
- **Termination**: Notice periods must be reasonable; **tacite reconduction** (automatic renewal) heavily regulated for B2C.

#### Data Processing Agreement
- **EU GDPR**: Applies directly.
- **CNIL**: Commission Nationale de l'Informatique et des Libertés - French supervisory authority.
- **Cookies**: Requires explicit consent (CNIL guidelines very strict).
- **DPO**: Required for public authorities and systematic large-scale processing of sensitive data.
- **Breach Notification**: 72 hours to CNIL (Article 33 GDPR).

### Practical Guidance
- Statuts should be drafted in French for registration with Registre du Commerce et des Sociétés (RCS).
- SAS provides maximum flexibility for startup governance and preference shares.
- Ensure BSPCE/BSA issuance complies with tax rules (Art. 163 bis G CGI for BSPCE).
- For employees: French labor law is protective; dismissal requires **cause réelle et sérieuse** (real and serious cause).

## Czech Republic (CZ)

### Applicable Law
- **Zákon č. 90/2012 Sb., o obchodních korporacích (ZOK)** - Act on Business Corporations
- **Zákon č. 89/2012 Sb., občanský zákoník (NOZ)** - Civil Code (contract law)
- **Zákon č. 182/2006 Sb., insolvenční zákon** - Insolvency Act
- **Zákon č. 256/2004 Sb., o podnikání na kapitálovém trhu (ZPKT)** - Capital Market Undertakings Act
- **EU GDPR** (directly applicable)

### Corporate Structure
- **s.r.o.** (společnost s ručením omezeným) - limited liability company (most common for startups)
- **a.s.** (akciová společnost) - joint-stock company (for larger companies/IPOs)

**Key Points**:
- **Společenská smlouva** (articles of association) for s.r.o.; **Stanovy** (articles) for a.s.
- Minimum capital: CZK 1 for s.r.o. (since 2014); CZK 2,000,000 for a.s.
- Notarization required for certain documents (e.g., articles).
- Commercial Register (Obchodní rejstřík) registration mandatory.

### Fundraising Pack Compliance

#### Founders' Agreement / Shareholders' Agreement
- **Form**: Shareholders' agreement ("smlouva akcionářů" or "smlouva společníků") enforceable under NOZ contract law.
- **Pre-emption Rights**: Statutory for s.r.o. unless excluded in společenská smlouva (§§211-212 ZOK).
- **Drag-Along**: Enforceable if contractually agreed.
- **Restrictive Covenants**: Non-compete clauses subject to reasonableness test under NOZ. Must be proportionate in scope, duration, geography.
- **Preference Shares**: Permitted for a.s. (§278 ZOK); limited for s.r.o.

#### SAFE / Convertible Instruments
- **Not Common**: SAFEs rare in Czech Republic. Alternatives:
  - **Směnitelné dluhopisy** - convertible bonds (for a.s.).
  - **Opční podíly** - option agreements for s.r.o. shares.
- **Prospectus**: EU Prospectus Regulation applies; ZPKT implements rules.
- **Capital Contributions**: §§142-143 ZOK (s.r.o.), §§246-250 ZOK (a.s.) - shares must be issued for adequate consideration.
- **Notarization**: Certain corporate actions require notarial deed.

### SaaS Pack Compliance

#### SaaS Subscription Agreement
- **NOZ Contract Law**: §§1724 et seq. NOZ (contracts).
- **B2B**: Generally enforceable if commercially reasonable.
- **B2C**: Consumer protection applies (Zákon č. 634/1992 Sb., o ochraně spotřebitele); 14-day withdrawal right for distance contracts.
- **Unfair Terms**: §§1813-1814 NOZ regulate unfair terms in consumer contracts.
- **Liability**: Cannot exclude liability for gross negligence or willful misconduct.

#### Data Processing Agreement
- **EU GDPR**: Applies directly.
- **ÚOOÚ**: Úřad pro ochranu osobních údajů (Office for Personal Data Protection) - Czech supervisory authority.
- **Breach Notification**: 72 hours to ÚOOÚ (Article 33 GDPR).
- **Language**: Czech language recommended for enforceability and ÚOOÚ proceedings.

### Practical Guidance
- All official documents should be in Czech (for Commercial Register and ÚOOÚ).
- s.r.o. provides adequate flexibility for most startups; a.s. required for IPO.
- Ensure společenská smlouva permits transfers and drag-along provisions.
- Czech courts may be unfamiliar with SAFE-style instruments; convertible loans more common.
- For employees: Czech labor law protective; employment contracts must be in writing.

## General Compliance Principles

### Across All Jurisdictions

1. **Local Counsel Review Essential**: Always have documents reviewed by qualified legal counsel admitted to practice in the applicable jurisdiction.

2. **Notarization Requirements**: Many civil law jurisdictions (DE, FR, CZ) require notarization for share transfers and certain corporate actions. Factor in time and cost.

3. **Language**:
   - English templates may need translation for enforceability.
   - Official filings (company register, tax authorities) often require local language.

4. **Currency**: Specify currency clearly. Exchange rate fluctuations can affect economic terms.

5. **Tax Implications**:
   - Corporate tax varies widely (UK 25%, DE ~30%, FR ~25%, CZ 21%, US-DE 21% federal + state).
   - Withholding taxes on dividends, interest, royalties.
   - Transfer pricing for intra-group transactions.
   - VAT/GST for services (standard rate: UK 20%, DE 19%, FR 20%, CZ 21%).

6. **Data Protection**:
   - EU/UK: GDPR compliance mandatory.
   - US: State-by-state patchwork; no federal law (yet).
   - Consider data localization requirements (e.g., Russia, China if expanding).

7. **Employment Law**:
   - EU jurisdictions: highly protective of employees.
   - US: at-will employment (except Montana); less protective.
   - Restrictive covenants: enforceability varies (CA prohibits non-competes; DE/UK/FR/CZ allow if reasonable).

8. **Dispute Resolution**:
   - Common law (UK, US-DE): litigation-friendly; discovery process.
   - Civil law (DE, FR, CZ): less discovery; faster trials.
   - Arbitration: ICC, LCIA, AAA - enforceable under New York Convention.

9. **Force Majeure**:
   - Common law: requires contractual clause.
   - Civil law: may have statutory doctrine (e.g., France: **force majeure**; Germany: **Unmöglichkeit**).

10. **Electronic Signatures**:
    - EU: eIDAS Regulation - qualified electronic signatures (QES) have same legal effect as handwritten.
    - US: ESIGN Act and UETA - electronic signatures generally valid.
    - UK: Electronic Communications Act 2000 + eIDAS (as retained).

### Specific Template Categories

#### Fundraising Documents
- **Securities Laws**: Always check if offering requires registration or exemption.
- **Investor Accreditation**: Ensure investors meet accreditation requirements.
- **Anti-Money Laundering**: KYC/AML checks for investors.
- **Capitalization Table**: Keep accurate and up-to-date; use specialized software (Carta, Pulley).

#### SaaS Documents
- **Terms of Service**: Must be binding; ensure proper acceptance mechanism (clickwrap, signature).
- **Privacy Policy**: Must be transparent and compliant with GDPR/CCPA/etc.
- **Cookies**: Requires consent in EU/UK (PECR, ePrivacy Directive); implied consent may suffice in US.
- **Service Credits**: SLA breaches typically remedied by service credits, not damages (limit liability).

### Red Flags to Watch For

1. **"Boilerplate" Risks**: Don't copy-paste clauses without understanding jurisdiction-specific requirements.
2. **Outdated Templates**: Laws change; ensure templates reflect current law.
3. **Over-Broad Restrictive Covenants**: May be unenforceable and antagonize employees/founders.
4. **Unclear Liquidation Waterfall**: Causes disputes in exit; be precise.
5. **Missing GDPR Clauses**: Non-compliance risks fines up to €20M / 4% turnover.
6. **Inadequate Limitation of Liability**: May be struck down as unfair; ensure reasonable.

## Template-Specific Compliance Notes

### SAFE Agreement (Post-Money)
- **Jurisdictions**: Best suited for US-DE. Less common in UK/EU (use convertible loan notes instead).
- **Key Risks**:
  - Not regulated as security in most jurisdictions (but check).
  - Dilution calculations must be clear.
  - Ensure Certificate/Articles authorize conversion shares.

### Founders' Agreement
- **Jurisdictions**: Universal, but vesting mechanics vary.
- **Key Risks**:
  - Leaver provisions must be reasonable (restraint of trade).
  - Acceleration on exit may trigger tax (consult tax advisor).
  - IP assignment: ensure all pre-existing IP disclosed and properly assigned.

### Shareholders' Agreement
- **Jurisdictions**: Universal for post-investment governance.
- **Key Risks**:
  - Drag-along: ensure minority protection not breached.
  - Reserved matters: too restrictive = deadlock; too loose = loss of control.
  - Anti-dilution: full ratchet is rare and founder-unfriendly; broad-based weighted average is standard.

### SaaS Subscription Agreement
- **Jurisdictions**: Universal with overlays.
- **Key Risks**:
  - B2C requires different terms (consumer protection).
  - Export controls (if software restricted - encryption, defense).
  - Open source license compliance (if using FOSS).

### Data Processing Agreement (DPA)
- **Jurisdictions**: EU/UK GDPR-compliant; adapt for other jurisdictions.
- **Key Risks**:
  - International transfers: SCCs/IDTA required.
  - Sub-processors: must maintain list and allow objection.
  - Breach notification: 72-hour deadline is tight.
