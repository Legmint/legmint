## ‚úçÔ∏è Voice & Tone

### Writing Principles
1. **Friendly, not formal** ‚Äî "Start minting" not "Commence document generation"
2. **Active, not passive** ‚Äî "Mint your docs" not "Documents are generated"
3. **Clear, not complex** ‚Äî "Legal docs for startups" not "Legal documentation solutions"
4. **Confident, not boastful** ‚Äî "Lawyer-reviewed templates" not "The world's best platform"

### Voice Characteristics
- **Professional but never stiff** ‚Äî We know our stuff, but we're not stuffy
- **Slightly playful** ‚Äî "Mint" theme allows for creativity without being unprofessional
- **Empathetic** ‚Äî We understand founder pain points
- **Transparent** ‚Äî We're honest about limitations (this is NOT legal advice)

### Example Copy

#### Good Examples ‚úì
- "Legal docs, minted for startups"
- "Mint your legal foundation"
- "Start minting your first document"
- "Interactive templates designed for founders"
- "From incorporation to funding and beyond"

#### Avoid ‚úó
- "Revolutionary legal technology"
- "Disruptive AI-powered platform"
- "Enterprise-grade legal solutions"
- "Synergistic document workflow"

### Brand Vocabulary

#### Use These Terms:
- Mint / Minting (for document creation)
- Interactive templates
- Founders / Startups
- Legal foundation
- Incorporate, raise, hire, scale
- Lawyer-reviewed
- Startup-ready

#### Avoid These Terms:
- Generate / Creation (use "mint" instead)
- Clients / Customers (use "founders")
- Legal technology / LegalTech jargon
- Disrupt / Revolutionary
- AI-powered (unless specifically relevant)
